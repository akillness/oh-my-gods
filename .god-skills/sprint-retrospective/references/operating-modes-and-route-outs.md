# Operating Modes And Route Outs

Use this file when the request sits near another PM workflow or the
retrospective format is unclear.

## Mode chooser

Pick one dominant mode before writing the packet.

| Mode | Use when | Bias |
|------|----------|------|
| `sprint-retro` | One sprint or iteration just ended | Keep evidence close to sprint goal, carried work, bugs, and blocked items |
| `milestone-retro` | A milestone, release push, launch beat, or delivery arc just completed | Use timeline-plus-themes if the period was messy or cross-functional |
| `release-retro` | The team needs a broader reflection after shipping | Include approval friction, rollout issues, and support/feedback signals |
| `async-retro` | Team is distributed or cannot meet live | Favor short prompts and visible response deadlines |
| `team-reset` | The retro ritual itself is stale, blame-heavy, or actionless | Review why prior retros stopped being useful before choosing a new format |

## Route-out map

Do not force a retrospective when the real job belongs elsewhere.

| If the user mainly needs... | Route to... | Why |
|-----------------------------|-------------|-----|
| Daily coordination, blockers, or a short team sync | `standup-meeting` | That is execution cadence, not completed-work reflection |
| Scope breakdown, backlog shaping, or "what do we do next?" planning | `task-planning` | The team still needs ready work, not retro facilitation |
| Story points, t-shirt sizes, or forecast confidence | `task-estimation` | That is sizing work, not reflection |
| Log triage, outage forensics, or technical root cause isolation | `debugging` or `log-analysis` | A retro can follow later, but evidence collection comes first |

## Format chooser

Use the lightest structure that matches the team's needs.

| Format | Best for | Watch out for |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Start / Stop / Continue | Quick general retros | Can become generic if evidence is weak |
| 4Ls | Learning-heavy teams or milestone reviews | Keep "longed for" from turning into a wishlist |
| Mad / Sad / Glad | Emotion-heavy or morale-sensitive periods | Avoid venting without action narrowing |
| Timeline + themes | Complex releases, launches, or multi-team delivery | Do not turn the timeline into a minute-by-minute transcript |
| Action-review reset | Teams with recurring stale action items | Name why actions died before adding replacements |

## Async facilitation rules

- Ask for short responses with a deadline
- Use one response template for everyone
- Review prior actions before collecting new ones
- Cluster repeated responses into themes instead of listing each comment
- End with 2-3 owned actions and one follow-up checkpoint
