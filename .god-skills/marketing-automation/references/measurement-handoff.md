# Measurement and Handoff Rules

`marketing-automation` should leave a small but durable measurement plan in
every output.

## Minimum measurement payload

- Primary KPI: one number that matters most now
- Leading signal: early sign that the work is helping
- Baseline: current state or explicit assumption
- Success threshold: what improvement would count as a win
- Owner: who should implement or read the result next

## By lane

### CRO

- KPI: conversion rate, signup completion, checkout completion, CTA CTR
- Leading signal: CTA clicks, form starts, step completion rate
- Handoff: page or flow owner plus experiment backlog

### Copy and messaging

- KPI: CTR, reply rate, conversion rate, demo-booking rate
- Leading signal: scroll depth, email opens, CTA clicks, qualitative resonance
- Handoff: copy owner, designer, lifecycle marketer, or PMM

### SEO and content

- KPI: ranking, organic sessions, assisted conversions, qualified traffic
- Leading signal: indexation, impressions, click-through rate, internal-link
  coverage
- Handoff: content or SEO owner with page brief or outline

### Ads and analytics

- KPI: CAC, CTR, CPA, tracked conversion rate, clean event coverage
- Leading signal: event firing, audience match, campaign tracking hygiene
- Handoff: analytics owner or paid media operator with spec or checklist

### Strategy and growth

- KPI: launch conversion, activation, retention, trial-to-paid, churn reduction
- Leading signal: milestone completion, launch asset readiness, first-week
  funnel movement
- Handoff: launch owner, PMM, growth lead, or lifecycle owner

## Anti-patterns

- Do not leave measurement blank just because the user asked for copy.
- Do not list ten KPIs; keep one primary KPI and one leading signal.
- Do not assume implementation ownership is obvious; name the next owner or
  workflow.
