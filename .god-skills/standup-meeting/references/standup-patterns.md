# Standup Patterns

## Core idea

A useful standup is a coordination checkpoint for active work, not a planning
meeting or a management-performance ritual.

## Common standup modes

- Daily sync: short live check-in during active delivery
- Walk the board: board-first review of work by status
- Async check-in: written update for distributed teams
- Blocker triage: focused sync for stuck or risky work
- Cadence reset: redesign of a standup that has become noisy or wasteful

## Repeated failure modes

1. Status theater: updates are aimed at management instead of coordination
2. Person-first drift: the team narrates activity instead of reviewing work in
   motion
3. Planning leakage: backlog shaping or estimation crowds out daily delivery
4. Hidden blockers: dependencies are implied but not named or owned
5. Endless deep dives: the standup absorbs problem-solving that should happen
   after the sync

## Format selection cues

### Use daily sync when

- the team already knows the work
- the main need is alignment for today
- blockers are manageable and short to explain

### Use walk the board when

- the tracker is the real source of truth
- too much work is in progress at once
- review-ready and blocked items are more important than individual updates

### Use async check-in when

- the team is distributed across time zones
- a live meeting adds more friction than value
- written clarity matters more than conversation

### Use blocker triage when

- one or two blocked items threaten the sprint or milestone
- ownership or escalation is unclear
- the team needs follow-up actions more than broad status

## Transferable standup sequence

1. Restate the sprint or milestone goal
2. Review work in motion
3. Surface blocked or review-ready items
4. Assign follow-up owners
5. End before planning or problem-solving starts
